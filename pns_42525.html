The National Weather Service office in Phoenix, Arizona (PSR) will undergo scheduled AWIPS (Advanced Weather Interactive Processing System) upgrades beginning Monday, April 28, 2025, through Wednesday, April 30, 2025. This is part of a national upgrade program affecting NWS offices across the United States.
                
During this period, some services and products may be temporarily unavailable or handled by backup offices. This includes NOAA Weather Radio transmitters KEC94 (Phoenix, AZ), WWG42 (Globe, AZ), and WXL87 (Yuma, AZ/Imperial, CA). Based on similar recent upgrades at other offices (such as TWC and FFC), it is expected that NOAA Weather Radio will broadcast an "Off The Air" message stored in the AWIPS system during the outage.
                
We will update station statuses with the tag "AWIPS Not Operational" once the outage is confirmed. Some climate data products and radar feeds may also be intermittently unavailable during this upgrade window.
                
In addition, we expect the NWS Flagstaff (FGZ) office to undergo the same AWIPS upgrade process within the next month.
                
WSLF Radio weather products will not be affected during this time.  
For the latest weather information, please visit weather.gov/psr.
