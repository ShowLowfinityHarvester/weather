Hazardous Weather Outlook  
WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team – Phoenix, AZ  
5/13/2025 @8:05 AM MST
                 
For the following counties: in Arizona: Maricopa, Northwest Pinal, Southern Gila, La Paz, and Yuma; and the following counties: in California: Imperial, Eastern Riverside, and Extreme Eastern San Bernardino
                 
...For Today and Tonight...  
Temperatures will remain above normal today across the lower deserts with highs reaching the upper 90s to low 100s. Areas of Moderate HeatRisk persist across south-central Arizona. Residents should continue taking heat precautions such as staying hydrated, limiting strenuous outdoor activity, and dressing appropriately for the heat. Breezy to windy conditions are expected, especially during the afternoon and evening hours. Gusts of 25-40 mph will be common, with stronger gusts up to 45 mph in western Imperial County and up to 55 mph in far southwestern areas. A Wind Advisory remains in effect for all of western Imperial County through early Wednesday morning. 

In addition, very low relative humidity and dry fuels will combine with these winds to produce elevated fire weather conditions across the region. A Red Flag Warning is in effect this afternoon and early evening for southern Gila County and higher terrain areas of northeastern Pinal and eastern Maricopa Counties. Blowing dust may locally reduce visibility across southeast California.

The system will bring an increase in high and mid-level cloud cover, but dry weather will continue.

No thunderstorms are forecast

                 
...Looking Ahead For The Next 7 Days...  
Wednesday Through Monday...A cooler air mass will arrive Tuesday and Wednesday, leading to high temperatures falling 10–15° compared to today. Highs will range from the upper 70s to mid 80s in the higher elevations, and mid 80s to around 90 in the lower deserts. Windy conditions will persist through Tuesday, particularly in southern California and the higher terrain areas. Red Flag Warnings may be needed again for Tuesday due to continued fire weather concerns. 

By late week, temperatures are expected to rebound closer to seasonal normals under zonal flow aloft. Dry conditions are forecast to continue into the weekend. Model guidance suggests another trough may approach the West Coast by late weekend, but confidence in timing and intensity still remains low.

                 
...Spotter Information Statement...  
Spotter activation is not anticipated at this time. Spotters are encouraged to report any significant weather to the WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team and the NWS forecast office in Phoenix.
