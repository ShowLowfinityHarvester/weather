Hazardous Weather Outlook  
WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team Phoenix AZ  
5/4/2025 @12:48 PM MST  

For the following counties: Maricopa, Northwest Pinal, Southern Gila (AZ); La Paz, Yuma (AZ); Imperial (CA); Eastern Riverside, Extreme Eastern San Bernardino (CA)  

...For Today and Tonight...  
An unseasonably strong low pressure system will impact the region today, bringing widespread showers and scattered thunderstorms along with breezy to locally windy conditions. Some thunderstorms this afternoon and evening may produce gusty winds in excess of 40 mph and brief heavy rainfall, particularly across eastern Riverside and La Paz Counties.  

A Wind Advisory is in effect for eastern Riverside County through 2 AM PDT Monday. Gusts of 25 to 30 mph are expected, especially in and around the Imperial Valley.  

...Looking Ahead For The Next 7 Days...  
Monday through Wednesday will feature continued chances for scattered showers and isolated thunderstorms, especially across the Arizona high terrain, with lingering activity most likely Monday afternoon. Temperatures will remain well below normal through Tuesday, with widespread 70s across the lower deserts and highs barely reaching 70°F in some areas on Monday. Drier conditions are expected by midweek, followed by a warming trend Thursday through the weekend. Highs are forecast to return to the 90s by Thursday and may exceed 100°F by next weekend, leading to a potential period of Moderate HeatRisk for lower elevation zones. Fire weather concerns will remain limited early in the week due to elevated humidity levels, but breezy conditions and dry fuels may still support localized issues, particularly in southeast California. Elevated fire weather risk could return by late week as warmer and drier conditions develop.  

...Spotter Information Statement...  
Spotter activation is not anticipated at this time. Spotters are encouraged to report significant weather to the WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team and NWS Phoenix. Reports can also be shared on Twitter using #AZWX or #CAWX.
