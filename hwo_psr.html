Hazardous Weather Outlook  
WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team – Phoenix, AZ  
5/11/2025 @8:07 AM MST
                
For the following counties: in Arizona: Maricopa, Northwest Pinal, Southern Gila, La Paz, and Yuma; and the following counties: in California: Imperial, Eastern Riverside, and Extreme Eastern San Bernardino
                
...For Today and Tonight...  
Seasonably hot temperatures will persist across the lower deserts today with highs reaching the 100-108°F range. Widespread Moderate HeatRisk and localized Major HeatRisk are expected across the region. People should take standard heat precautions such as staying hydrated, limiting outdoor activity, and wearing light clothing. Breezy conditions are also forecast, with gusts of 20-30 mph possible, particularly across south-central and southwestern Arizona and southeastern California. Some increase in high cloud cover is expected but should not significantly impact temperatures. No other hazardous weather is anticipated.

                
...Looking Ahead For The Next 7 Days...  
A gradual cooling trend is expected through midweek as a dry low-pressure system approaches the region. High temperatures will fall below normal by Tuesday and Wednesday. Breezy to windy conditions are expected Monday and Tuesday, with gusts reaching 25-40 mph across the area and up to 55 mph in far southwestern Imperial County, and a Wind Advisory in effect there from 5 AM PDT Monday to 5 AM PDT Wednesday. Dry conditions are forecast to continue into next weekend.

                
...Spotter Information Statement...  
Spotter activation is not anticipated at this time. Spotters are encouraged to report any significant weather to the WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team and the NWS forecast office in Phoenix. Reports can also be shared on social media using #AZWX or #CAWX.
