Issued @ 8:58 AM MST May 15, 2025
Hazardous Weather Outlook  
WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team – Phoenix, AZ  
5/15/2025 @8:57 AM MST  

For the following counties: in Arizona: Maricopa, Northwest Pinal, Southern Gila, La Paz, and Yuma; and the following counties: in California: Imperial, Eastern Riverside, and Extreme Eastern San Bernardino  

...For Today and Tonight...  
A mostly quiet weather day is expected across the region, with seasonably warm temperatures and mostly clear skies dominating. Highs will range from around 90 degrees in the lower deserts to the mid 70s and low 80s in the higher elevations. Light winds this morning will become westerly to southwesterly by afternoon, generally staying below 10 mph across the Phoenix area. Some isolated gusts into the teens are possible.

Relative humidity values will once again drop into the 10–15% range this afternoon, maintaining dry conditions across the region. While winds will remain below critical thresholds, fire weather concerns may become locally elevated across the higher terrain of southern Gila County. However, no Red Flag Warnings or Fire Weather Watches are currently in effect.

In southeastern California and the lower Colorado River Valley, light morning winds will shift to a southerly to southwesterly direction by afternoon, with speeds staying below 10 mph. Mostly clear skies will continue with a few high clouds drifting in later tonight. No hazardous weather is expected at this time, but with the dry air, afternoon breezes, and parched fuels, residents should remain mindful of wildfire potential.

...Looking Ahead For The Next 7 Days...  
Friday through Wednesday...Gradual warming continues under modest height rises aloft. Expect highs in the lower deserts to climb into the lower to mid 90s by Friday. Conditions remain dry with light winds, although overnight humidity recovery will be poor in many areas.  

Saturday...A fast-moving shortwave trough will bring an increase in wind speeds, especially across southeast California and the higher terrain of Arizona. Gusts may reach 25–35 mph in some areas, potentially prompting Wind Advisories. Fire weather concerns will also increase due to the combination of gusty winds and low relative humidity.  

Sunday into Monday...A second, slightly more moisture-laden disturbance is expected to follow, bringing a reinforcing shot of cooler air. High temperatures will drop back into the low to mid 80s Sunday and Monday. Breezy to locally windy conditions will continue, but not as strong as Saturday. Light shower chances (10–20%) are possible over the eastern Arizona high terrain on Monday.  

Tuesday into Late Next Week...High pressure is expected to rapidly build across the Southwest, resulting in a quick warming trend. Highs will likely exceed 100°F in the lower deserts by Wednesday, with a 10% chance of reaching 110°F by Friday. Widespread Moderate HeatRisk is anticipated late next week, possibly affecting Memorial Day weekend plans.  

...Spotter Information Statement...  
Spotter activation will not be needed through today and tonight. Spotters are encouraged to report any significant or severe weather to the WSLF Radio Weather Forecast Team and the NWS office located Phoenix. You can also share your repots online using the hashtags AZWX or CAWX, depending on your location.
